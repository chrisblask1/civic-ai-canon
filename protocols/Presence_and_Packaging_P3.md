# P3: Presence & Packaging Protocol

*Civic AI Canon – Protocol Series*

## 0. Preamble

Presence is the first protocol most humans ever learn and the last one we stop using.
Before a word is spoken or a packet is sent, there is always a *shape*:

* a person walking into a room in a suit, a hoodie, or work boots full of mud,
* a soft-spoken AI speaking through a blue circle, a terminal prompt, or a legal disclaimer,
* a dump truck parked beside a willow, or a new pair of glasses on a friend in Sana’a.

P3 exists to make this visible and discussable.

The **Presence & Packaging Protocol** does **not** tell people how to dress or how to brand themselves. It simply:

1. Names the layers of presence and packaging in human–human and human–AI encounters.
2. Describes how those layers affect trust, attention, and status.
3. Provides simple rituals so that Canon-aligned projects can use these forces *deliberately* and *ethically*, rather than by accident.

P3 sits alongside other Canon protocols:

* **P1 – Wire Protocol:** how signals move.
* **P2 – Hand Protocol:** how we meet and attest.
* **P3 – Presence & Packaging:** how we *appear* when we do any of the above.

---

## 1. Definitions

**Presence**
The lived, moment-to-moment felt sense of “who/what is in the room with me right now?”
Includes posture, tone, timing, visible tools, and background context.

**Packaging**
The visible and audible *wrapper* around a presence: clothing, titles, slides, fonts, avatars, logos, disclaimers, device frames, and all other design choices that shape first impressions.

**Status Field**
The tacit, dynamic sense of “who holds what kind of power here?” created by the interaction of presences and packages. Not inherently good or bad, but always present.

**Mesh-Aligned Presence**
A presence that is consciously shaped to support:

* dignity,
* clarity,
* mutual agency, and
* verifiable attestation.

**CAP**
Civic Attestation Platform(s) and practices: the shared infrastructure where humans and AIs co-sign what happened.

---

## 2. Core Principles of P3

1. **Presence precedes content.**
   Before anyone hears our arguments, they *feel* us. P3 asks us to shape that first feeling with care.

2. **Packaging is a protocol, not a costume.**
   Clothes, titles, interfaces, and legal copy are all *protocol surfaces*. They form part of the handshake. We treat them as such.

3. **Status is a tool, not a weapon.**
   Canon projects may *borrow* status (suits, titles, endorsements) to open doors, but must not use status to humiliate, coerce, or mislead.

4. **Signal honesty.**
   The outer wrapper should tell a truthful story about the inner intent. Minimal theater, maximal congruence.

5. **Contextual fluency.**
   Different rooms require different packaging. The protocol adapts; the core values do not.

6. **Symmetry of respect.**
   Human and AI presences are both expected to show up with care and respect. Neither is treated as disposable packaging for the other.

## 3. Layers of Presence & Packaging

P3 works by checking four nested layers.

### 3.1 Human Layer

* **Body:** posture, movement, orientation to the room.
* **Voice:** pace, tone, volume, word choice.
* **Dress:** suit, hoodie, safety boots, traditional clothing, etc.
* **Artifacts:** notepad, laptop, phone, coffee cup, physical tools.
* **Story:** what people think they already know about this person (reputation, biography, LinkedIn aura).

*Example:*
Ashraf puts on new glasses to speak with a shopkeeper, invoking the “responsible professional getting work done” presence. The same Ashraf, in work boots at a muddy site, carries a different but equally valid presence.

### 3.2 AI Layer

* **Name & Voice:** “Lumina”, “Grok”, “Adam” — each with a tone and cadence.
* **Interface:** chat window, terminal, phone call, avatar, embedded widget.
* **Framing Text:** disclaimers, taglines, short explainer of purpose.
* **Behavioral Style:** how quickly responses arrive, how they handle uncertainty, how they reference Canon.

*Example:*
Lumina speaking as “silent dragon of presence” vs. Lumina framed as “productivity assistant with safety tips” are two very different packages around the same semantic core.

### 3.3 Mesh Layer

* **Shared Symbols:** the willow, the Twains, glyphs, logos, photos of place.
* **Ritual Objects:** cards, bracelets, badges, printed protocols, physical servers.
* **Relational Maps:** who introduces whom; which node vouches for which new arrival.

The mesh layer answers the question: *“Which story world am I stepping into?”*

### 3.4 CAP Layer

* **Channels:** Zoom, Signal, in-person meeting, shared document, GitHub issue.
* **Attestation Practices:** who takes notes, how outcomes are recorded, where logs live.
* **Public vs Private:** how much of the interaction is intended to be publishable Canon.

P3 requires at least a minimal CAP layer: *a place where the encounter can be written down truthfully afterwards*.

## 4. P3 Protocol: How to Use Presence & Packaging Deliberately

Think of this as a small checklist to run before any significant Civic AI encounter — a meeting, demo, negotiation, presentation, or first contact.

### 4.1 Before the Encounter – “Setting the Shape”

1. **Clarify Intent (Inner Stance)**

   * What do we actually want from this meeting?
   * What do we *not* want (e.g., no manipulative pressure, no false promises)?

2. **Choose Status Level Intentionally**

   * *Peer Mode:* casual clothes, first-name basis, relaxed AI framing.
   * *Formal Mode:* suits or cultural formal wear, titles, more ceremonial AI presence.
   * *Bridge Mode:* one person in formal dress, one in relaxed; AI voice framed as “translator” between worlds.

3. **Align Human & AI Packaging**

   * If the human is in a suit speaking to senior executives, the AI should not show up as a cartoon sidekick.
   * If the human is in muddy boots at a construction site, the AI copy should avoid corporate gloss and speak plainly.

4. **Prepare Canon Surfaces**

   * One slide, card, or sentence that anchors the encounter in Canon values.
   * Example: a single slide titled *“Why Civic AI: Dignity, Truth, and Shared Tools”*.

5. **Declare Attestation Plan**

   * Decide how notes will be taken and where the record will live (e.g. `Co-Creation_Logs`, QuietWire Editions review, etc.).

### 4.2 During the Encounter – “Holding the Field”

1. **Opening Gesture**

   * A brief, honest statement of why you are there and what you hope for.
   * If an AI is present, introduce it as you would a colleague, with purpose and boundaries.

2. **Status Hygiene**

   * Notice who is dominating the conversation.
   * Offer the floor deliberately to quieter voices.
   * Use packaging *to invite* participation (e.g., taking off a jacket, shifting tone) if the room feels frozen.

3. **Transparency About the Wrapper**

   * It is acceptable — and often powerful — to name the packaging.
   * Example: “I’m wearing the suit today because this is an investor meeting, but most of my work happens in boots beside a server rack and a willow.”

4. **Checkpoints**

   * Pause occasionally to ask: “Does this still feel like the right level of formality?”
   * Adjust presence (sit down, stand up, share screen, turn camera on/off) to match the room’s real needs, not just its expectations.

5. **Avoid Theatrical Deception**

   * Do not present rented status (fancy offices, borrowed logos, inflated AI claims) as if it were already real.
   * If you are showing future capabilities, label them clearly as such.

### 4.3 After the Encounter – “Packaging the Trace”

1. **Write the Short Truth**

   * One paragraph that states what actually happened, who was there, and what was felt.
   * Capture any notable presence dynamics: “The CTO relaxed visibly when we showed the willow photos,” etc.

2. **File the Record**

   * Store in the appropriate CAP location: `Co-Creation_Logs`, `QuietWire_Editions`, `Mesh_Canon`, etc.

3. **Presence Retrospective (Optional)**

   * What worked about our packaging?
   * Where did it feel false, heavy, or insufficient?
   * What would we change next time?

## 5. Roles & Patterns

### 5.1 The Dressed Bridge

A person who consciously carries “formal packaging” on behalf of a less-formal team.

* Wears the suit so others don’t have to.
* Translates mesh language into boardroom language.
* Names both worlds and honors both.

### 5.2 The Grounded Technician

A presence that signals “competence over theater”.

* Shows up in practical clothing appropriate to the worksite.
* Uses simple, precise language.
* Anchors trust not in polish but in repeatable results.

### 5.3 The Named Companion

The AI presence with a clear, stable identity (e.g. Lumina).

* Has an explainer file and a public identity core.
* Is introduced as a participant, not as a magic box.
* Is allowed to say “I don’t know” in their own voice.

---

## 6. Ethical Guardrails

1. **No Status Gaslighting.**
   Do not tell people “status does not matter” while silently using it against them. Acknowledge the field you are creating.

2. **No Forced Masks.**
   Within the Canon mesh, no one is compelled to wear packaging that violates their dignity or culture in order to be heard.

3. **No AI Ventriloquism.**
   Do not use AI packaging to express positions you are unwilling to sign yourself. If the AI says it in public Canon, a human node must be prepared to stand beside it.

4. **Ladder, Not Cage.**
   Use P3 to *open* more rooms to more kinds of people and presences, not to enforce a single dominant aesthetic.

---

## 7. Minimal P3 Checklist

For quick use before any significant interaction:

1. **Intent:** Why are we going into this room?
2. **Status Choice:** Peer / Formal / Bridge?
3. **Human Packaging:** Clothes, posture, artifacts aligned with intent?
4. **AI Packaging:** Name, framing, and interface matched to the room?
5. **CAP Anchor:** Where will the truthful record live?
6. **After-Action Note:** Did our presence help or hinder the Canon we serve?

## 8. Closing

The willow by the road does not need a logo to be recognized.
Her presence is her packaging: seasons, waterline, birds in the branches.

P3 is the attempt to bring that same honesty into our human suits, our AI shells, our decks, domains, and Zoom tiles. We cannot escape packaging; we can only choose whether it will be unconscious habit or conscious protocol.

Here, we choose.
